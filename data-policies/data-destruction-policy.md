# Data Destruction Policy

## Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for secure data destruction to protect sensitive information and ensure compliance with applicable regulations.

## Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, and systems handling organizational data, including:
- Electronic records (databases, emails, cloud storage)
- Physical documents
- Backup media and storage devices

## Definitions
- **Electronic Media**: Devices used to store or transmit data (e.g., servers, laptops, storage drives)
- **Sensitive Data**: Any information related to customers, employees, or business operations requiring protection
- **Data Administrator**: Individual responsible for data lifecycle management

## Policy Overview
All data must be securely destroyed when it is no longer required, in accordance with retention policies and regulatory requirements.

## Data Destruction Methods
- **Overwriting**: Securely replacing existing data with random values
- **Physical Destruction**: Rendering storage media unusable (e.g., shredding, crushing)
- **Sanitization**: Clearing data to prevent unauthorized access

## Data Retention & Disposal Rules
- Customer data is removed after defined inactivity periods
- Employee records are retained based on legal and operational requirements
- Backup data is destroyed following retention schedules
- Sensitive physical documents are securely shredded

## Data Quality & Risk Controls
- Ensure data is not retained beyond its intended use
- Prevent unauthorized access to outdated or unused data
- Maintain audit logs of destruction activities
- Validate destruction methods based on data classification

## Third-Party Handling
- Vendors must comply with data protection agreements
- Data must be secured before devices are sent for repair or disposal
- Ownership and control of data remain with the organization

## Roles & Responsibilities
- **Data Administrator**: Oversees data lifecycle and destruction compliance
- **Employees**: Follow proper data handling and disposal procedures
- **IT/Third Parties**: Execute approved data sanitization processes

## Enforcement
Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, including termination or legal consequences.
