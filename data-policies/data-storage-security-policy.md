# Data Storage and Security Policy

## Purpose
Define standards for secure data storage, retention, and access to protect sensitive information and ensure data integrity.

## Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, systems, and data generated within the organization, including:
- Electronic records (databases, cloud systems, emails)
- Physical documents
- Images, audio, and digital assets

## Definitions
- **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**: Data that can identify an individual
- **Sensitive Data**: Confidential business, employee, or customer information
- **End User**: Any individual interacting with organizational data

## Data Storage Standards
- Data must be stored on secure, access-controlled systems
- Access is limited based on role and business need
- Sensitive data must be encrypted in transit and at rest

## Allowed Data Storage
- Customer contact and transaction data
- Employee records (as required for operations)
- Vendor and contractor information

## Prohibited Data Storage
- Personal passwords or PINs
- Unencrypted payment information
- Unauthorized or non-consented data

## Data Retention Policy
- Daily incremental backups and weekly full backups
- Data retained only as long as necessary for business or legal purposes
- Outdated or unnecessary data must be securely removed

## Data Security Controls
- Multi-factor authentication required
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Automatic session timeouts
- Encryption of sensitive data
- Separation of business and personal device usage

## Data Quality Standards
- Accuracy: Data must reflect true values
- Completeness: Required fields must be populated
- Consistency: Standard formats enforced
- Timeliness: Data updated promptly
- Integrity: Data protected from unauthorized changes

## Monitoring & Auditing
- Regular audits of data access and storage practices
- Review of data accuracy and integrity
- Logging of system activity and changes

## Roles & Responsibilities
- **Data Administrator**: Oversees data storage, retention, and compliance
- **Employees**: Follow data handling and security protocols
- **IT/Support**: Maintain secure systems and enforce controls

## Enforcement
Policy violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination or legal consequences.
